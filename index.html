Protein target is 175g daily. Between food and your 2-scoop shake with oats (~42–45g P), you're consistently hitting this. Supplements below are additional support on top of food and shakes.
⚠ Check your multivitamin's magnesium content. You're already taking 300mg magnesium glycinate at night. If the multi contains 100mg+, reduce your evening dose to stay within the 200–400mg daily total target. Also note collagen is not a complete protein — do not count it toward your 175g target.
⚡ Creatine Monohydrate
5g daily
With post-workout shake on training days. With breakfast on Sunday.
Supports strength output and recovery. Every day including rest day.
☀️ Vitamin D3
1,000–2,000 IU daily
Take with Vitamin K2 and a fat-containing meal — fat-soluble, requires dietary fat for absorption.
D3 increases calcium absorption. K2 (below) ensures that calcium goes to bones, not arteries. The two work as a pair.
🦴 Vitamin K2 (MK-7)
100–200mcg daily
Take alongside Vitamin D3 with a fat-containing meal.
MK-7 form only — longer half-life than MK-4. Directs calcium to bones and teeth rather than blood vessels. Essential companion to D3 supplementation.
🐟 Omega-3 Fish Oil
1–2g EPA/DHA daily
With D3 and K2 at the same meal — all three are fat-soluble.
Anti-inflammatory, joint health, cardiovascular and brain function benefit.
✨ Collagen Protein
10–15g daily (1 scoop)
Mid-morning or afternoon snack — always alongside Vitamin C. Do not take post-workout (whey handles that).
Hydrolysed bovine Type I & III. NOT a complete protein — do not count toward 175g target. Low in tryptophan. Vitamin C is required as a cofactor for collagen synthesis — without it absorption is significantly reduced.
🍊 Vitamin C
500–1,000mg daily
Take with collagen protein at mid-morning or afternoon snack.
Primary role here is collagen synthesis cofactor. Also supports immune function and iron absorption. Use ascorbic acid (cheap, effective) or buffered calcium ascorbate if 1,000mg causes stomach discomfort.
🔩 Zinc
15–25mg daily
With food — zinc on an empty stomach causes nausea. With lunch or dinner.
Zinc citrate or zinc picolinate only — zinc oxide is poorly absorbed. Do not exceed 40mg/day consistently as it competes with copper absorption. Supports testosterone production, immune function and recovery.
💊 Male Multivitamin
Per label (1–2 tablets daily)
With breakfast or your largest meal.
Look for: B complex (B6, B12, folate), selenium, lycopene, no added iron (men rarely need it — excess is harmful). Recommended: Vitabiotics Wellman, Myprotein Alpha Men, or NOW Foods ADAM. Check magnesium content and adjust evening dose accordingly.
🌙 Magnesium Glycinate
200–300mg every evening
With cottage cheese at 9:00pm — every night.
Reduce to 200mg if your multivitamin contains 100mg+ magnesium. Target 200–400mg total daily from all sources. Supports deep sleep and overnight muscle recovery.
